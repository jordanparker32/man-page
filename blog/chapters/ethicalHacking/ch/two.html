The Transport Layer
  • Hackers - access computer system or network without authorization. (This is illegal.)
  • Crackers - break into systems to steal or destroy data. The U.S. Department calls both hackers.
  • Ethical Hacker - performs most of the same activities with owner's permission.
  • Script kiddies or packet monkeys - younger, inexperienced hackers who copy codes from knowledgable hackers.
  • Programming languages used by experienced pen testers - Python, Ruby, Practical Extraction and Report Language (PERL), C language.
  • Script - set of instructions. Runs in sequence to perform tasks.
  • Hacktivist - a person who hacks computer systems for political or social reasons.

  • Penetration testers usually have - a laptop with multiple OSs and hacking tools.

  • Job requirements for a pen tester might include:
    • Perform Vulnerability, attack, and penetration assessments in intranet and wireless environments.
    • Perform discovery and scanning for open ports.
    • Apply appropraite exploits to gain access.
    • Participate in activities involving application penetration.
    • Produce reports documenting discoveries.
    • Debrief with the client at the conclusion.
Penetration-Testing Methodologies
  • White box model - tester is told about the network topology and technology, may even be given a floor plan. The tester is permitted to interview IT personnel and company employees. This makes the testers job easy
  • Black box model - staff does not know about the test. Tester is not given details about technologies used. Tests security personnel's ability to detect an attack.
  • Gray box model - hybrid of the white and black box models. Company gives the tester partial information (like what OS is used but no network diagram).
Certification Programs for Network Security Personnel
  • Certification programs - available in almost every area of network security.
  • Minimum certification - CompTIA Security+ or equivalent knowledge.
